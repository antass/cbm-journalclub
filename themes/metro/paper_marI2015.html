March 2015

Presenter: Liz Moses

Experimental Paper

Abstract

Background: Agents targeting programmed death-1 receptor (PD-1) and its ligand (PD-L1) are showing promising results in non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC). It is unknown whether PD-1/PD-L1 are differently expressed in oncogene-addicted NSCLC.
Methods: We analysed a cohort of 125 NSCLC patients, including 56 EGFR mutated, 29 KRAS mutated, 10 ALK translocated and 30 EGFR/KRAS/ALK wild type. PD-L1 and PD-1 expression were assessed by immunohistochemistry. All cases with moderate or strong staining (2 þ /3 þ ) in 45% of tumour cells were considered as positive.
Results: PD-1 positive (þ) was significantly associated with current smoking status (P1⁄40.02) and with the presence of KRAS mutations (P 1⁄4 0.006), whereas PD-L1 þ was significantly associated to adenocarcinoma histology (P 1⁄4 0.005) and with presence of EGFR mutations (P 1⁄4 0.001). In patients treated with EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors (N 1⁄4 95), sensitivity to gefitinib or erlotinib was higher in PD-L1 þ vs PD-L1 negative in terms of the response rate (RR: P 1⁄4 0.01) time to progression (TTP: Po0.0001) and survival (OS: P 1⁄4 0.09), with no difference in PD1 þ vs PD-1 negative. In the subset of 54 EGFR mutated patients, TTP was significantly longer in PD-L1 þ than in PD-L1 negative (P 1⁄4 0.01).
Conclusions: PD-1 and PD-L1 are differentially expressed in oncogene-addicted NSCLC supporting further investigation of specific checkpoint inhibitors in combination with targeted therapies.

D'Incecco et al., British Journal of Cancer
